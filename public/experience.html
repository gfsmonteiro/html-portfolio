Gus Monteiro's Experience

December 2014 – May 2022
Engineering Manager, Attractions Technology
Universal Orlando Resort
Orlando, FL - USA

Started as Senior Network Security Engineer, Supervisor in 2016, Manager in 2018. Created the UO Attractions Technology team from scratch based on NIST frameworks to deploy, secure and support 14 interconnected Operations Technology (OT) networks, servers, firewalls and virtualization infrastructure in support of the industrial control systems (ICS) that operate UO attractions across three theme parks.

Created and implemented policies and procedures to formalize security workflows (change management, access control, vulnerability management) as well as the Attractions Technology Business Continuity Plan.

Contributing member of Universal Orlando Engineering’s Policy Committee, and point person for internal/external cybersecurity audits, management action plans and capital projects progress.

Responsible for all aspects of UO attractions cybersecurity, including network security controls such as access lists and traffic encryption with IPSec, access management via AAA and RADIUS/TACACS+ servers plus Microsoft Active Directory and LDAP, Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), password management, etc.

Implemented secure remote access to OT networks and ICS systems using PasswordState, with differentiated access permissions based on Microsoft AD groups and with session recording and logging.

Created a NOC/SOC function with automated workflows for account provisioning, just-in-time privilege escalation, deprovisioning, inactivity alerts and account expiration/disabling, network and services monitoring and alerts plus automated backups with periodic manual offline backups for added protection.

Created a roadmap for the evolution of OT networks that included extensive automation and the use of private cloud technologies based on MAAS and OpenStack to achieve scaling with security while keeping OT domain isolated from IT.
